RE: Formal Notice of Disputed Outgoings & Rent Review — Shop 4, 123 Collins Street, Melbourne

Dear Sir / Madam,

We write on behalf of Coastal Brew Co. Pty Ltd ("Tenant") in respect of the above premises held pursuant to the Retail Lease dated 1 March 2022 ("Lease").

Following a forensic review of the FY2023–24 outgoings reconciliation statement and rent invoices, the Tenant has identified the following discrepancies totalling $4,235 in overcharges, which we are instructed to dispute:

1. Incorrect CPI Rent Review ($935)
The rent review dated 1 March 2024 applied a CPI increase of 4.2%. Pursuant to clause 5.3(b), the applicable index is the Melbourne All Groups CPI (ABS Catalogue 6401.0). The correct figure for the relevant period (Q4 2023) is 3.1%, resulting in an overcharge of $935 for the year.

2. Metropolitan Improvement Levy in Land Tax ($1,200)
The FY2023–24 land tax reconciliation includes a metropolitan improvement levy of $18,780 (Tenant's share: $1,200). Clause 10.1(b) of the Lease expressly excludes metropolitan levies and surcharge assessments from recoverable land tax. This charge is not permitted.

3. Capital Expenditure in Outgoings — Lift Motor Replacement ($2,100)
The FY2023–24 outgoings statement includes "Lift motor replacement" of $45,000 (line item 14). Pursuant to clause 9.2(c), items with a useful life exceeding five years are excluded from recoverable outgoings. A lift motor has a typical useful life of 15+ years and is a capital item. The Tenant's proportional share of this charge ($2,100) is disputed.

The Tenant requests that the Landlord:

(a) Issue a revised FY2023–24 reconciliation statement reflecting the above corrections;
(b) Credit or refund the total overcharge of $4,235 within 30 days of this notice; and
(c) Provide full documentary evidence (including the SRO land tax assessment notice and lift contractor invoices) within 14 days to allow verification.

The Tenant reserves all rights under the Lease and the Retail Leases Act 2003 (Vic), including the right to refer this matter to the Victorian Small Business Commission if not resolved.

We look forward to your prompt response.

Yours sincerely,

[Tenant's Solicitor / Authorised Representative]
